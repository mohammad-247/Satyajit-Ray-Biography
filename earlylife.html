Early Life

Satyajit Ray was born on May 2, 1921, in Calcutta (now Kolkata), into a highly intellectual and culturally rich Bengali family. His lineage was filled with artists, writers, and thinkers who greatly influenced Bengal’s literary and artistic renaissance.

His grandfather, Upendrakishore Ray Chowdhury, was a noted writer, illustrator, and publisher who founded the children’s magazine Sandesh. His father, Sukumar Ray, was a legendary Bengali poet and humorist, famous for his nonsensical rhymes in Abol Tabol — works still loved by generations.

Tragically, Sukumar Ray passed away when Satyajit was just two years old, leaving his mother Sujata Ray to raise him alone. Despite facing financial hardships, Sujata ensured Satyajit received a strong education and exposure to the arts.

He attended Ballygunge Government High School and later studied Economics at Presidency College, Calcutta, graduating in 1940. Though he studied economics, his interests leaned toward art, music, and literature.

After graduation, Ray joined Visva-Bharati University in Santiniketan, founded by Rabindranath Tagore. Initially hesitant, he later described his time there as life-changing. He was deeply influenced by teachers like Nandalal Bose and Benode Behari Mukherjee, who taught him the value of observing rural life and appreciating Indian art and nature.

Ray later said that his stay at Santiniketan helped him discover India — the India beyond cities, full of simplicity, beauty, and human stories — a perspective that became the foundation of his filmmaking style.
